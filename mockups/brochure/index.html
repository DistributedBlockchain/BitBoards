Billboard vacant?

Traditional advertising is failing because the consumer's attention has moved to Facebook.

As our society becomes more reliant on the mobile web, billboards along the highways are becoming more vacant. Fewer companies want to keep throwing money at a problem that doesn't have the sales conversion rates they're looking for.

Companies have to rely on spectacle, invocative (sometimes provocative) imagery, and monopolization to capture attention in the physical world. These strategies still have a high rate of failure because the consumer's attention has moved to the phone.

What if we could deliver content to a specialized group of people that couldn't be ad-blocked, was relevant to a group who'd be interested in such products, and at a fraction of the cost?
